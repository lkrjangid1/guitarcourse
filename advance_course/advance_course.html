A six-stage course · for the working guitarist

Stop reading chord sheets.
Start hearing them.

A practical course in playing by ear. By the end, you'll hear a song and find its chords, sing your own melody and pick chords that fit it, and choose strumming patterns by feel instead of memorisation — live, in real time, on the spot.

What you'll actually walk away with

1.

Find any song's key

Hear a song on the radio — within 30 seconds, know which key it's in and which chords are available to you.

2.

Sing & accompany yourself

Hum a melody, find the chord that supports each phrase, change chords where the song wants to change.

3.

Pick strumming by mood

Match rhythm to feel — not from a memorised list, but by hearing the song's pulse.

4.

Improvise live

Sit in any jam session, hear two bars, and play the rest. The freedom every guitarist actually wants.

How this course is different

Most guitar courses give you chord sheets and ask you to memorise more of them. This course doesn't add chords to your vocabulary — it teaches you to hear what chord the song wants, then play it. The chords you already know are enough; what changes is your ear.

The six stages

Each stage is one HTML file. Start with Stage 1 and move forward — they build on each other. Each stage takes about a week of practice to internalise before the next one will fully click.

Before you begin

Prerequisites
  • You can hold and play the basic chords: G, C, D, Em, Am.
  • You can switch between them without too much fumbling.
  • You can play 2–3 simple strumming patterns to a beat.
  • You're willing to sing out loud, even badly. This is non-negotiable — your ear improves only when your voice is involved.
Mindset

You're not building more knowledge. You're building connections between what your ear hears and what your hands do. This is slow at first and then suddenly fast — like learning to swim. Stick with the drills even when they feel pointless.

Honest expectation

Stage 1 alone will not make you play by ear. You need all six stages plus consistent practice — figure 6–12 weeks total if you practice 20 minutes a day. The good news: the curve is steep at the start, and progress is obvious to you.

How to use each stage

  1. Read the stage in full once, without your guitar. Get the concepts.
  2. Read it again with your guitar. Try every example.
  3. Do the drills. Every stage has drills — they're not optional, they're the whole point.
  4. Apply to a song you already know. Each stage ends with a "real-world application" section.
  5. Don't move to the next stage until the drills feel easy. Rushing wastes everything.
